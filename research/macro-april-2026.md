## 1. RATE ENVIRONMENT

### Where We Are in the Cycle
The Federal Funds Rate sits at **3.50%–3.75%** as of April 2026. The Fed cut three times in 2025 (25bp each), then paused. The December 2025 dot plot showed one more cut penciled in for 2026, targeting a floor around 3.25%.

**Current status: Late cutting cycle, effectively in a holding pattern.**

### FOMC April 29–30 Meeting
- **Hold probability: 94.8%** (CME FedWatch, ~2 weeks ago)
- **Any change probability: 5.2%**
- The April meeting is a non-event on the rate decision. The market action is in the press conference and forward guidance language.

### The Fed's Bind
The Fed cannot cut aggressively because:
1. **CPI is at 2.6% YoY** — close to target but not there
2. **Core PCE is stuck at 2.8%** — above the 2% target
3. **Tariffs are inflationary** — the new 10% baseline duty + sector tariffs will push CPI higher in Q2-Q3 2026. The Tax Foundation estimates $600/household cost increase.
4. **Labor market is resilient** — March payrolls came in strong (rebound from February softness)

The Fed cannot hike because:
1. Growth is slowing
2. Tariff-driven slowdown is a supply-side shock (hurts both inflation AND growth)
3. Political pressure from the administration (though Powell will hold the line)

**Bottom line:** Fed is on hold through mid-2026 at minimum. Markets are pricing in 1-2 cuts by year-end — June and/or July as the base case (~28-45% probability). Don't bet on anything before June.

### Rate Cycle Positioning
We are in a **late-cycle pause** — not a new hiking cycle, not a full-speed easing cycle. This environment historically favors:
- Quality over junk
- Profitable tech over unprofitable growth
- Dividend payers / cash flow generators
- Gold and Treasury inflation protection

## 2. DOLLAR STRENGTH / WEAKNESS

### Current Dollar Setup
The USD is in a conflicted position:
- **Bearish forces:** Tariff uncertainty is creating "sell America" flows (Politico, Jan 2026 warned of this). Foreign investors reducing US asset exposure = dollar weakness pressure
- **Bullish forces:** US economy still outperforming global peers (Goldman forecasts 2.6% US GDP vs 2.0% consensus); rate differential still favors USD vs EUR and JPY

### Assessment
**USD is range-bound, with a mild weakening bias over Q2 2026.** The tariff shock is broadly bad for the dollar because it reduces capital inflows and raises questions about US policy credibility. But the dollar doesn't collapse because there's no credible alternative reserve currency.

**Trading implications:**
- Dollar weakness → tailwind for BTC, gold, commodities, international equities
- Watch EUR/USD: a break above 1.12 signals meaningful dollar weakness and risk-on rotation into international assets
- Watch DXY: holding ~103-105 = neutral; break below 100 = meaningful dollar weakness play confirmed

## 5. OVERALL POSITIONING THESIS

### **Selective Aggression**

Not aggressive (too much macro uncertainty). Not defensive (economy isn't breaking, AI spend is real, crypto cycle isn't done). **Selective aggression** — pick your spots, size appropriately, stay hedged.

### Playbook:

**OVERWEIGHT:**
- Defense (RTX, LMT) — structural budget tailwind, tariff-exempt sector
- AI infrastructure (NVDA) — capex commitments locked, buy the tariff dip
- BTC — buy dip in post-halving cycle, not selling until $100K+ reclaim or cycle indicators turn
- Long volatility — tariff uncertainty isn't going away, VIX calls pay
- Gold/TLT — Fed-on-hold + dollar weakness + uncertainty = store-of-value bid

**UNDERWEIGHT:**
- Consumer discretionary — tariff tax hits wallets, Q2 guidance will get cut
- Retail / Apparel — direct tariff victims on import costs
- Pharma — 200% tariff threat is an Overhang no one wants to hold into
- High-yield credit — spreads should widen if growth slows

**AVOID:**
- Biotech (regulatory + tariff risk combined)
- Chinese ADRs (trade war escalation risk)
- Over-leveraged balance sheets in any sector

### The Bull Case (40% probability)
Tariffs become negotiating chips, get walked back by H2 2026. Fed cuts twice (June + September). S&P 500 hits 7,500. BTC reclaims $100K on its way to a cycle top. AI earnings continue to beat.

### The Bear Case (25% probability)
Tariffs stick, CPI spikes to 3%+, Fed can't cut. Consumer spending cracks. S&P 500 tests 2025 lows. BTC breaks $60K. Recession call in Q3-Q4 2026.

### The Base Case (35% probability)
Muddle-through. Tariff partial rollback. Fed holds, then cuts once in September. Markets trade sideways with elevated volatility. Individual stock selection and sector rotation matters more than beta.

**Probability-weighted: selective aggression, asymmetric plays, stay hedged. This is not a buy-everything market.**
